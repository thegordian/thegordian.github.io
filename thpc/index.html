Transport hubs are most cost-effective if they are located at places that are along many movement traces and are either close to where the traces start, end or merge. Such hubs represent minimal detours and are attractive for travelers. Try to beat Gordian by selecting road segments for potential hub locations that are within a user-defined distance from the start of as many movement traces as possible. As a little hint: A segment is brighter the more movement traces pass through it. A triangle is larger the more movement traces start from it. Good luck!
